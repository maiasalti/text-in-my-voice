# Voice profile — how I text

> This is a TEMPLATE. Replace it with a description of **your** texting style —
> either generate it automatically with `python build_voice_profile.py`, or fill
> in the sections below by hand.
>
> The watcher feeds this file to Claude as the system prompt. The more specific
> and honest it is about your real habits (even the "bad writing" ones), the more
> the drafts will sound like you. When in doubt, describe what you *actually* do,
> not what's "correct".

## Capitalization
- e.g. Mostly lowercase? Sentence case? ALL CAPS for emphasis? Do you ever
  capitalize "correctly", or basically never?

## Punctuation
- e.g. Do you end texts with a period (or never)? `!!` vs a single `!`? Where do
  question marks go? Em-dashes, ellipses — yes or no?

## Length & rhythm
- e.g. Short one-liners? Several quick texts in a row instead of one paragraph?
  Long blocks only when something's serious?

## Slang, fillers, recurring phrases
- e.g. Your shorthand (`u`, `ur`, `gonna`, `lmk`, `dw`, `omw`, `ngl`…), reactions
  (`omg`, `lol`, `bet`), and any signature words or endearments you use a lot.

## Emoji & emoticons
- e.g. Which emoji actually show up, how often, and where (usually at the end?).
  Do you lean on `:)` / `<3` / `xx` more than emoji?

## Tone & register
- e.g. How your voice shifts: chaotic/crude with close friends, neutral-warm with
  acquaintances, softer for affection, calmer and fuller for serious moments.
